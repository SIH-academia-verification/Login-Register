Backend Verification Engine

OCR & Anomaly Detection

Our advanced backend engine uses Optical Character Recognition (OCR) to extract data from uploaded certificates. It then employs sophisticated anomaly detection algorithms to identify any inconsistencies or potential signs of fraud, ensuring the initial document's integrity before it's even considered for the blockchain.

Blockchain Hash Check

Immutable Record Creation

Once a certificate is verified, a unique cryptographic hash (a digital fingerprint) is generated. This hash is then recorded on a public or private blockchain, such as the Ethereum testnet or Hyperledger Fabric. This creates an immutable, timestamped record of the certificate's authenticity.

Verification Process

To verify a certificate, its hash is recalculated and compared against the one stored on the blockchain. A match confirms the certificate is authentic and has not been altered since its issuance, providing unparalleled trust and security.
